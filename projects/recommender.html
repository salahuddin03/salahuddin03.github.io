Object Recognition and Shape Analysis for Automated Conveyor Belt Operations

  • Technology: Object recognition, shape analysis, and convolutional neural networks (CNNs)

This project's main goal was to create an automated conveyor belt system that could improve material handling procedures. The system could recognize things on the conveyor belt, classify them using object identification and form analysis, and then modify the speed and direction of the belt to best facilitate the movement of materials. The technology's capability to recognize risks and automatically stop the belt to prevent accidents increased safety while also increasing system efficiency.

To build the system's object detection and classification capabilities, the project used a convolutional neural network. The system was put to the test under various circumstances, and the findings revealed a notable improvement in efficiency and a decrease in expenditures. The system's capacity for a variety of industrial applications was proved by its ability to precisely handle several goods of varied sizes and forms. In conclusion, this project demonstrated the benefits of incorporating form analysis and object recognition into automated material handling systems, paving the way for safer and more effective industrial operations.
