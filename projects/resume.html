Shadow Analysis and Energy Optimization of a Building Using SketchUp and TRNSYS

  • Technologies: SketchUp, TRNSYS, TRN3D

This project's goal was to use the simulation tools TRNSYS and TRN3D to assess a building's energy efficiency. I began by creating the structure in SketchUp and simulating the building's shadow in TRN3D under various weather scenarios. The building's energy efficiency was then automatically modeled by TRNSYS after the TRN3D file was imported. I was able to assess numerous energy-saving strategies using the TRNSYS software, including daylighting, shading, and passive solar heating and cooling. Based on the simulation results, I changed the architecture of the building and performed the simulation once again to get even more precise results.

For studies on solar access and shadow analysis, the TRN3D program proved to be a useful tool that gave me knowledge about the structure's solar potential. In order to determine the best energy-saving tactics, the simulation results were examined, and the building's energy performance was then optimized. Overall, this study showed how well TRNSYS and TRN3D work for analyzing the energy performance of buildings. By comparing alternative energy-saving tactics, the software enabled me to maximize the building's energy performance, resulting in a more environmentally friendly and effective building design.
