    1. Techno-Economic and Environmental Feasibility Analysis of Solar water Heating System for a Textile Industry In Bangladesh

    Technology: Flat Plate thermal Collector, Boiler, Feed water tank

    Project Link

    From a thermal, economic, and environmental standpoint, this article investigates the viability of using solar thermal energy in Bangladesh's textile sector. A review of solar collectors is included in the study in order to choose the best one, and MATLAB is used to model the flat plate solar collector in great detail. Different settings are changed to examine the collector's thermal performance.

    To ascertain the financial viability and environmental impact of integrating solar collectors in the textile sector, economic and environmental evaluations are also carried out.

    According to the study, installing solar collectors can result in energy savings of up to 14.7% in the summer and about 9% in the winter, with a payback period of 13.58 years. The environmental study indicates that this is an environmentally favorable approach from the Bangladeshi context since the quantity of CO2 emission that can be decreased is around 2.8 tons per day in the summer and 1.65 tons per day in the winter.


    2. Computational Modeling of Fire Dynamics for Enhanced Fire Protection and Design

    Technology: Pyrosim, SketchUp

    The project involves modeling a building in SketchUp and simulating the behavior of the building after a fire in PyroSim. SketchUp, a 3D modeling program that enables the construction of thorough and precise architectural designs, was used to produce the building's initial design. The building was then imported into PyroSim, a piece of software intended to model the behavior of fire and smoke in structures.

    The building was set up for fire simulation after being imported into PyroSim by entering the fire characteristics, such as the ignition source, fire size, and fuel load. The software was then ran to simulate the fire and smoke behavior within the building. The simulation results were examined to see if the building's fire protection precautions were sufficient and to spot any potential problem areas. Making informed judgments regarding the building's design and construction was made possible thanks to the use of PyroSim, which enabled a more thorough and accurate evaluation of the building's fire protection measures.

In order to determine the best energy-saving tactics, the simulation results were examined, and the building's energy performance was then optimized. Overall, this study showed how well TRNSYS and TRN3D work for analyzing the energy performance of buildings. By comparing alternative energy-saving tactics, the software enabled me to maximize the building's energy performance, resulting in a more environmentally friendly and effective building design.

    7. Generation of Small amount of Electric Energy by using Noise (Sonic Energy)

    Technology: Piezoelectric, electrodes

    A unique method for producing electric energy from environmental sources like noise is to use piezoelectric devices. The foundation of this technology is the idea of piezoelectricity, which explains a material's capacity to generate an electric charge in response to mechanical stress. The technique has been looked at as a potential source of renewable energy and is employed in many devices, including sensors and actuators.

    Piezoelectric devices are used to produce electricity from sound by sandwiching a piezoelectric material, usually a crystal, between two electrodes. The distortion of the crystal structure caused by the noise vibrations striking the piezoelectric material separates positive and negative charges, creating an electric potential difference throughout the material. The electrodes then gather this charge, producing a tiny amount of electrical energy. Even though this technique only produces a tiny quantity of electricity, it is a cutting-edge strategy that could aid in the production of sustainable energy.
